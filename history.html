Before analyzing the impacts of their gender issues, here are some background information about the Aztec's history.
The Aztec civilization appeared in Mesoamerica in the early 13th century. Their origins are not exactly determined
but they are believed to begin as a northern tribe of hunter-gatherers whose name came from their homeland Aztlan.
Tenochtitlan, the Aztecs emerged as the dominant force in central Mexico, developing an intricate social, political,
religious and commercial organization that brought many of the region's city-states under their control by the 15th
century. But then on February 1521, they were colonized by a Spanish conqueror called Hernan Cortes due to the glory
and power and wealth.

       
       

Even so, they have contributed in our society even today. Some examples are their contribution to pharmacy and education.
They are one of the first empires to require mandatory education to children. They also created medicines used to prevent
muscle spasms and reduce pain.
