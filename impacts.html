The roles and duties for the people in the past were affected which resulted to certain differences among them.

- Women were to raise the daughters and men were to raise the sons.

- Daughters become female priests or cihuatlmacazqui and accompany male tasks by spinning, weaving clothing,
and cleaning the temple.

- Men occupied roles that would allow them to accomplish tasks that would demonstrate their masculinity
(such as succession in hunting) among the opposite gender.

- When it comes to marriage, the newlywed will always move in the groom's family and the males could take
as many secondary wives or concubines as they could sustain.

And when it comes to authorities and such, the role of tribal leaders pertained to the men most of the time, yet, women
(that are particularly elder) took the religious leadership roles and even served in tribal meetings.

This has also impacted our society today as stated before. Femininity and masculinity were socially constructed. This
results in further sexism that we know of today especially in the media as well as toxic masculinity and gender
expectations from people. The inequality of marriage also often results to toxic households and abuse. Though the good
news is that women now have more rights than before.
